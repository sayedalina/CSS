battle of karbala


Karbala is a holy city in Iraq and one of the most important religious sites for Muslims, especially Shia Muslims. It is famous because of the Battle of Karbala, which took place on 10 Muharram, 61 AH (10 October 680 CE).



Background of the Battle:


After the death of Muawiya I, his son Yazid I became ruler of the Islamic empire.


Imam Hussain ibn Ali, the grandson of Prophet Muhammad, refused to pledge allegiance (Bay'ah) to Yazid because he believed Yazid's rule was unjust.


Many people from Kufa invited Imam Hussain to lead them. Imam Hussain left Mecca with his family and companions and traveled toward Kufa.



The Battle of Karbala:


When Imam Hussain's caravan reached Karbala, Yazid's army surrounded them and blocked access to water from the Euphrates River


On the 10th of Muharram, known as Ashura, Imam Hussain and his small group of companions faced a much larger army.


Key facts:


  • Imam Hussain had about 72 companions.
  • The opposing army numbered in the thousands.
  • Many of Imam Hussain's family members were martyred.
  • Even infants and young family members suffered greatly due to lack of water.


Important Martyrs of Karbala:


  • Imam Hussain ibn Ali
  • Abbas ibn Ali
  • Ali Akbar ibn Hussain
  • Ali Asghar ibn Hussain
  • Qasim ibn Hasan


Lessons from Karbala:


Karbala is remembered as a symbol of:

  • Truth over falsehood
  • Justice over oppression
  • Courage and sacrifice
  • Patience during hardship
  • Standing for principles even in difficult circumstances

Death with dignity is better than a life of humiliation.
